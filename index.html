Topic 1: Number and Algebra

Standard form • sequences & series • proof • complex numbers • induction
▼
1.1Standard form: Operate with numbers written as a×10^k
1.2Arithmetic sequences and series: Find terms and sums using d
1.3Geometric sequences and series: Find terms and sums using r
1.4Financial applications: Model compound interest and annual depreciation
1.5Integer exponents and basic logs: Use exponent laws, log₁₀ and ln
1.6Simple deductive proof: Structure clear LHS→RHS algebraic arguments
1.7Rational exponents and full logs: Apply log laws, solve exponentials
1.8Infinite geometric series: Sum convergent geometric progressions
1.9Binomial theorem (natural n): Expand (a+b)ⁿ using nCr and Pascal
1.10Counting and extended binomial: Use permutations, combinations, non-integer powers
1.11Partial fractions: Decompose rational expressions into simpler terms
1.12Complex numbers (Cartesian): Work with a+bi and Argand diagrams
1.13Polar and Euler forms: Express z as r cisθ and re^{iθ}
1.14Complex roots and De Moivre: Find powers and roots of complex numbers
1.15Induction and contradiction: Prove or refute general mathematical statements
1.16Linear systems: Solve up to three simultaneous linear equations

Topic 2: Functions

Graphs • transformations • polynomials • exponentials & logs
▼
2.1Straight line equations: Use gradient and intercept forms of lines
2.2Function concept: Define domain, range and informal inverse idea
2.3Graphs of functions: Sketch y=f(x) from descriptions or contexts
2.4Graph features: Identify extrema, zeros, symmetry and asymptotes
2.5Composite and inverse functions: Form f∘g and find f⁻¹(x)
2.6Quadratic forms and graphs: Rewrite, locate vertex and intercepts
2.7Quadratic equations/inequalities: Solve using formula, discriminant and signs
2.8Reciprocal and simple rational graphs: Analyze shapes and asymptotes
2.9Exponential and logarithmic graphs: Sketch, compare and interpret inverse pairs
2.10Solving equations: Solve analytically and via graph intersections
2.11Graph transformations: Translate, reflect and stretch basic functions
2.12Polynomial functions: Use factor/remainder theorems and root relations
2.13Further rational functions: Study behavior, asymptotes and restrictions
2.14Even, odd and self-inverse: Classify symmetry and find restricted inverses
2.15Inequalities between functions: Solve g(x)≥f(x) algebraically and graphically
2.16Modulus and reciprocal transformations: Graph |f(x)|, f(|x|), 1/f(x), [f(x)]²

Topic 3: Geometry and Trigonometry

3D geometry • trig identities • vectors • planes
▼
3.13D distance and solids: Find distances, midpoints, volumes and areas
3.2Trig in triangles: Use sine, cosine, tangent, sine rule, cosine rule
3.3Trig applications: Solve height, distance and angle problems
3.4Radians, arcs and sectors: Relate angle, arc length and sector area
3.5Unit circle definitions: Define trig ratios and exact special values
3.6Trig identities: Use Pythagorean and double-angle relationships
3.7Circular function graphs: Transform sine, cosine and tangent curves
3.8Solving trig equations: Solve within intervals, including quadratic forms
3.9Reciprocal and inverse trig: Graph sec, cosec, cot, arcsin, arccos, arctan
3.10Compound angle identities: Apply sin(A±B), cos(A±B), tan(2A)
3.11Trig symmetry and relationships: Use periodicity and symmetry in graphs
3.12Vector basics: Represent vectors, components and displacement in 2D/3D
3.13Scalar product: Compute dot product, angles, perpendicularity and parallelism
3.14Vector equation of a line: Use r=a+λb, parametric and Cartesian forms
3.15Relationships between lines: Classify coincident, parallel, intersecting, skew
3.16Vector product: Compute v×w and interpret magnitude geometrically
3.17Equations of planes: Write vector, scalar and Cartesian plane equations
3.18Intersections and angles in 3D: Solve line–plane, plane–plane intersections

Topic 4: Statistics and Probability

Data • distributions • regression • Bayes
▼
4.1Data and sampling concepts: Compare populations, samples, bias and outliers
4.2Presenting data: Construct histograms, cumulative curves and boxplots
4.3Central tendency and dispersion: Calculate mean, median, IQR, variance
4.4Correlation and regression (y on x): Interpret r and use regression line
4.5Basic probability concepts: Use sample spaces, events, complements, expectation
4.6Combined and conditional events: Use diagrams, independence, mutual exclusivity
4.7Discrete random variables: Define distributions and expected values
4.8Binomial distribution: Model fixed-trial success situations, mean, variance
4.9Normal distribution: Use normal curve and inverse normal calculations
4.10Regression of x on y: Find and interpret regression of x on y
4.11Conditional probability formulae: Apply definitions, test for independence
4.12Standardization with z-scores: Use z to find probabilities and parameters
4.13Bayes' theorem: Update probabilities for up to three events
4.14Variance and continuous variables: Work with PDFs, mean, variance, transformations

Topic 5: Calculus

Limits • differentiation • integration • DEs • series
▼
5.1Limits and derivative concept: View gradient as limiting rate of change
5.2Increasing/decreasing behaviour: Use sign of f′ to describe monotonicity
5.3Power rule basics: Differentiate polynomials of the form axⁿ
5.4Tangents and normals: Find equations using derivatives at points
5.5Basic integration as antiderivative: Find F(x) and simple areas under curves
5.6Differentiating standard functions: Use chain, product and quotient rules
5.7Second derivative and shape: Relate concavity and points of inflexion
5.8Extrema and inflection points: Optimize and classify stationary points
5.9Kinematics with calculus: Relate displacement, velocity and acceleration
5.10Integrating standard functions: Integrate using inspection and substitution
5.11Definite integrals and areas: Evaluate areas, including between curves
5.12Continuity and first principles: Understand limits, differentiability and base definition
5.13Advanced limits: Use l'Hôpital's rule and series for limits
5.14Implicit differentiation and rates: Solve related rates and optimization problems
5.15Advanced derivatives and integrals: Differentiate/integrate extended function families
5.16Integration techniques: Apply substitution and integration by parts repeatedly
5.17Areas and volumes of revolution: Find areas and solids of revolution
5.18First-order differential equations: Solve analytically and with Euler's method
5.19Maclaurin series: Expand standard functions and manipulate power series
